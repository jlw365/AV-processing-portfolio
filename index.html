Coursework Project: An Interactive Tool for FM and AM Visualization

Download!



For my final Advanced Audio-Visual Coursework Project, I chose to take what is typically thought of as an introductory audio processing concept and transform it into an in-depth, interactive audio-visualization tool illustrating frequency modulation (FM) and amplitude modulation (AM).

On the left half of the screen, you will see the FM modulation demonstration. The size of the modulating solid circle is controlled by the current output frequency, and the sizes of the outlined circles illustrate the frequencies of the carrier wave and the modulating wave. By manipulating the carrier frequency, modulation frequency, and modulation index, the user can visualize how each of these parameters directly affect the output sound.

On the right, the AM modulation demonstration allows the user to manipulate the carrier wave and modulating wave frequency, as well as a final amplitude multiplier to demonstrate the pulsing effects of basic AM. In this case, the amplitude now controls the size of the circles.

I have also implemented a saw-tooth, square, and triangle wave option for both modulation demos, which can be selected by holding down different keys listed on the screen and provide some interesting visual and audio results.

I hope that students and teachers alike will use this tool in the future to gain a deeper understanding of FM and AM modulation through this nuanced audiovisual representation.
